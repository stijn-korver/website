Process

How a project
actually works

Every engagement follows the same core progression — from problem understanding to a working solution you own.

01
Discovery

We map your problem, data landscape, existing systems, and success criteria. No assumptions, no skipping ahead.

02
Design

We propose an architecture — what gets built, what gets integrated, and what the end result looks like in your hands.

03
Build

We build iteratively, with regular check-ins. You see progress early and often, not just at the end.

04
Handover

Documentation, walkthroughs, and ongoing support options. You leave with something you fully understand and can operate.

Engagement Models

Work with us
on your terms

Every organisation is different. We offer four ways to engage, depending on what the problem and timeline demand.

Fixed-Scope Project
Defined deliverable, defined price

A clear problem, a clear output. We scope it together, agree on a price, and deliver. Works best when the problem is well-understood and the success criteria are specific.

Best for: Automation systems, classifiers, research reports, dashboards

Research-First
Understand before building

When you know there's a problem but aren't sure what the solution looks like yet. We investigate, prototype, and validate before committing to a full build.

Best for: Novel domains, feasibility questions, organisations new to AI

Embedded
Inside your team, temporarily

We join your team for a defined period — working alongside your people, in your stack, on your roadmap. Useful when you need technical depth without a permanent hire.

Best for: In-progress projects, capability gaps, R&D teams

Retainer
Ongoing technical partnership

A reserved capacity arrangement for organisations that need recurring AI support, model maintenance, or continued development beyond a first project.

Best for: Long-term integrations, evolving systems, continuous improvement

Why Custom?

Off-the-shelf tools have a
ceiling

Custom-built means the model is trained on your data, the interface is designed for your users, and the integration works with your systems — not the other way around.
